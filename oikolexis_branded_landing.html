About OikoLexis

A translation ecosystem, not just a tool

OikoLexis was born from a concrete request by the OHDSI Africa community for accessible translations of The Book of OHDSI. It has since grown into a language-agnostic, community-governed platform that respects both scientific precision and the autonomy of local language communities.

The project operates under the umbrella of the Research Group of Biomedical Data Sciences at Hasselt University (Belgium), in close collaboration with the global OHDSI network.

Core principle: AI augments experts — humans always decide. Every machine translation passes through structured peer review by bilingual, OHDSI-knowledgeable community members.

AI-powered machine translation

Multi-engine MT (GPT, DeepL, DeepSeek) with versioned prompts and transparent cost and quality metrics stored per job.

Structured expert review

Role-bound workflow with defined reviewer levels, enforced state transitions, and full audit trails. No revision step can be skipped.

Governance & traceability

Every decision, approval, and terminology choice is logged. GitHub stores text; PostgreSQL stores metadata and version history.

Language-agnostic by design

Built on IETF language codes and open standards. Any language community can join without infrastructure lock-in.

How it works

From source text to trusted translation

OikoLexis coordinates two phases: a preparative phase that structures the work, and a revision phase where community experts validate each translation unit.

01

Machine translation & chunking

The Book of OHDSI (Markdown/Bookdown on GitHub) is split into deterministic revision chunks. Each chunk is machine-translated using the best available engine for the target language.

02

Terminology governance

Scientific and technical terms are extracted per chapter, defined in context, and validated by chapter authors before translation. This lexical foundation guides the MT and ensures consistency.

03

Role-bound review workflow

Language-specific work groups — co-managers, editors, reviewers, supervisors — are assigned through OikoLexis. Each task binds to a role and transitions follow an enforced, audited workflow.

04

Bilingual revision matrix

Reviewers work in a paired source/target revision interface. Changes are committed to GitHub with full version traceability. No step can be skipped or performed out of sequence.

05

Published, community-owned translation

Approved translations are published under community governance — adding new languages, chapters, or reviewers requires no structural change to the platform.

Who we need

Roles in a language work group

The ideal reviewer is bilingual and has working knowledge of OHDSI concepts. Each role has a defined scope within the platform.

Co-manager / Co-editor

Coordinates the work group, oversees planning, approves role assignments and progress milestones.

Reviewer (levels 1–3)

Reviews machine-translated chunks against the source. Must be bilingual and familiar with OHDSI methodology.

Supervisor

Provides final approval on contested sections; typically a senior OHDSI community member in the target language.

Linguistic consultant

Advises on terminology and nuance. OHDSI knowledge is not required — expert linguistic profile is sufficient.
